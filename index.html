Language

Language

Blade Evidence

The complete image set, metadata, and annotations captured for a blade during a Blade Inspection.

AvoidInspection Evidence, Blade Image, raw upload, inspection note

Required Blade Image Slot

A numbered required image position for a Blade Inspection until field-specific view names are confirmed.

AvoidFake view name, inspection note, optional upload

Inspector

The on-site evidence capture role responsible for collecting complete Blade Evidence for assigned sites and turbines.

AvoidPilot, SME, defect reviewer

Technician

The field execution role assigned to a Work Order to perform the work, upload Repair Evidence, and mark the work done pending Repair Review and Work Order Verification.

AvoidInspector, SME, verifier, approver

SME

A remote subject-matter expert who performs Blade Reviews and Repair Reviews, creates Defect Annotations, and creates/submits Findings downstream with technical recommendations.

AvoidSite Manager, approver, technician, work order verifier

Finding

A formal defect finding explicitly created downstream by an SME from selected Defect Annotations in reviewed Blade Evidence.

AvoidInspection Note, defect ticket, issue

Finding Status

The lifecycle status of a Finding from SME drafting through closure or archival.

AvoidBlade Review Status, Work Order Status, defect status

Blade Review

An SME review of all Blade Evidence for one blade on one turbine, started automatically when the corresponding Blade Inspection is submitted.

AvoidImage verification, inspection note, assigned review case

Blade Review Status

A status summarizing the SME review progress and completion for one Blade Review. It does not track downstream Finding creation.

AvoidFinding status, image verification status, blade verified

Image Review Status

The SME completion status for one image inside a Blade Review.

AvoidDefect Annotation, Finding status, Blade Inspection Status

SME Review Queue

The set of automatically started Blade Reviews available to the SMEs assigned to a Site.

AvoidSME assignment, review case owner, assigned review ticket

Claim Blade Review

The action where an SME begins a Blade Review, making that SME the active reviewer and preventing other SMEs from working on the same review at the same time.

AvoidOperational assignment, Finding Approval, Work Order assignment

Release Blade Review Claim

The action that removes the active SME claim from a Blade Review so another assigned SME can claim it.

AvoidReopen Finding, Inspection Return, Work Order reassignment

Defect Annotation

A marked image region and classification created during a Blade Review.

AvoidDefect, Finding

Create Finding

The downstream SME action that turns selected Defect Annotations from reviewed Blade Evidence into a formal Finding.

AvoidAutomatic finding creation, Defect Annotation, Finding Approval

No Findings

A result meaning no formal Findings exist after reviewed Blade Evidence has gone through the downstream Finding creation path.

AvoidVerified without defects, closed

Project Manager

A programme coordinator who manages inspection and repair workflow across one or more sites.

AvoidSite Manager, site assignee

Managed Site

A site within a Project Manager's coordination scope.

AvoidAssigned site

Site Manager

The site-level operational authority for a wind site, also called a Site In-charge in Indian O&M operations. The product role remains SITE_MANAGER.

AvoidProject Manager, programme coordinator, site assignee

Admin

A company-level operational administrator responsible for tenant setup, users, sites, and fallback workflow actions inside their company.

AvoidSME, Site Manager, Project Manager, normal workflow owner

Super Admin

The Aflo platform governance role responsible for onboarding companies, platform-level configuration, reference data, and cross-company access setup.

AvoidAdmin, customer approver, workflow owner

Company

The tenant boundary for a wind asset owner, ISP/O&M provider, or Aflo-owned service organization whose users and data are scoped together.

AvoidSite, customer, client, account

Site

A wind farm or wind power plant location containing a fleet of turbines.

AvoidCompany, project, account, Work Order

Turbine

A single wind turbine generator within a Site. WTG is acceptable as a field label or industry abbreviation, but Turbine is the product term.

AvoidSite, blade, Work Order

Blade

A physical blade on a Turbine, usually identified as Blade A, Blade B, or Blade C.

AvoidTurbine, Finding, Work Order

Turbine Inspection

The inspection pass for one Turbine, normally made up of one Blade Inspection for each blade on that turbine.

AvoidBlade Review, Work Order, Site inspection

Blade Inspection

The Inspector field inspection pass for one Blade on one Turbine, producing Blade Evidence for SME review.

AvoidBlade Review, Finding, inspection note

Blade Inspection Status

A status summarizing Inspector capture and submission progress for one Blade Inspection. It only describes whether the required Blade Evidence has been uploaded and submitted; it does not describe defects or Findings.

AvoidBlade Review Status, No Findings, Findings Present, auto-completed inspection

Submit Blade Inspection

The Inspector action that confirms the uploaded Blade Evidence is correct and ready for SME review.

AvoidAuto-complete, Finding submission, SME submission

Inspection Return

An operational action that sends a submitted Blade Inspection back to the Inspector for correction with remarks when the Blade Evidence is not acceptable.

AvoidSME correction request, Blade Review rejection, Finding rejection

Inspection Remarks

Comments attached to a Blade Inspection or Inspection Return explaining evidence quality issues or inspection process notes.

AvoidDefect Annotation, Finding, SME recommendation

Completed Blade Inspection

A Blade Inspection where the Inspector has uploaded all three required blade images for that Blade and submitted the Blade Inspection for SME review.

AvoidUpload-only complete, Blade Review completed, verified blade, Finding

Completed Turbine Inspection

A derived completion state for a Turbine Inspection when every expected Blade Inspection for that Turbine is complete.

AvoidSeparate turbine submission, Completed Blade Review, Turbine Review, verified turbine

Turbine Inspection Status

A derived UI status summarizing the Blade Inspections for a Turbine. It is not a separate domain object, and it surfaces returned blade inspections.

AvoidTurbine Review Status, inspection case, inspection record

Completed Blade Review

A Blade Review where every required image has Image Review Status Review Complete. A completed review does not require formal Findings, and Defect Annotations do not need to be attached to Findings at review completion.

AvoidCompleted Blade Inspection, closure review, Work Order Verification

Blade Review Rollup

A derived UI status summarizing the expected Blade Reviews for a Turbine. It is not a separate domain object.

AvoidTurbine Review, Turbine Review Status, review case, review record

Finding Approval

An operational governance decision that accepts or rejects a submitted Finding for action. The normal owner is the Site Manager, with Project Manager and Admin as fallback or coordination roles.

AvoidSME approval, technical approval

Changes Requested

A Finding status meaning the operational approver returned the submitted Finding to the SME for edits and resubmission.

AvoidRejected terminal, Finding Approval, archived finding

Archived Finding

A terminal Finding state for duplicates, accidental submissions, or invalid Findings that should not continue through the workflow.

AvoidChanges Requested, rejected for edits, deleted finding

Work Order

An execution record that groups one or more approved Findings into assigned field work, an Operational Decision, Repair Evidence, Repair Review, and Work Order Verification.

AvoidAction Case, Closure Review

Work Order Status

The lifecycle status of a Work Order from creation through closure or archival.

AvoidFinding Status, Blade Review Status, Turbine Health Status

Work Order Creation

The operational action that creates a Work Order from one or more approved Findings.

AvoidProject Manager-only creation, Action Case creation

Repair Evidence

Pictures and proof uploaded against a Work Order to show the completed field work.

AvoidBlade Evidence, Blade Inspection, Work Order Verification

Repair Review

An SME technical review of Repair Evidence before final operational sign-off on a Work Order.

AvoidTurbine Review, Blade Review, Work Order Verification

Repair Review Status

A status summarizing the SME technical review outcome for Repair Evidence on a Work Order.

AvoidWork Order Status, Work Order Verification, Turbine Review Status

Operational Decision

The response chosen for approved Findings, such as repair, replace, monitor, reinspect, defer, or escalate to vendor.

AvoidAction Case, SME recommendation, Finding decision

Operational Decision Change

An audited change to the selected Operational Decision on an open Work Order.

AvoidNew Finding, silent decision edit, duplicate Work Order

Reinspect

An Operational Decision that schedules a future Blade Inspection to collect fresh Blade Evidence for a Finding.

AvoidMonitor note, defer, Blade Review

Monitor

An Operational Decision that keeps a Finding open for observation during a defined review window without scheduling a new Blade Inspection yet.

AvoidReinspect, defer, closed finding

Defer

An Operational Decision that acknowledges action is needed for a Finding but intentionally postpones it for operational reasons until a target date.

AvoidMonitor, Reinspect, no action

Closure Evidence

Evidence required to close a Work Order for non-repair Operational Decisions such as monitor, defer, or reinspect.

AvoidRepair Evidence, Blade Evidence, Closure Review

Pending Operational Review

A Work Order state for non-repair Operational Decisions that remain open until their monitoring window, defer target date, or reinspection schedule is resolved.

AvoidClosed, Technician assignment, Repair Review

Work Order Verification

The final sign-off on a completed Work Order, either accepting the work or sending it back for rework.

AvoidClosure Review, Repair Review, SME verification

Work Order Verification Status

The outcome of final operational sign-off on a Work Order.

AvoidRepair Review Status, Finding Approval, Closure Review

Turbine Health Status

A derived operational condition for a Turbine after inspections, reviews, Findings, Work Orders, Repair Reviews, and Work Order Verification have settled.

AvoidTurbine Review, Blade Review Status, Work Order status

  • A Technician does not create Findings, approve Findings, choose the Operational Decision, perform Repair Review, or perform Work Order Verification

  • A Blade Inspection is complete when all three required blade images are uploaded and the Inspector submits it for SME review

  • Returned for Correction means an Admin, Site Manager, or Project Manager returned the submitted Blade Inspection to the Inspector with Inspection Remarks
  • Blade Inspection Status never includes No Findings or Findings Present because the Inspector uploads all required Blade Evidence regardless of blade condition

  • An Inspection Return must include Inspection Remarks
  • An SME cannot create an Inspection Return, edit Blade Evidence, or control Blade Inspection Status

  • Any SME assigned to the Site can claim a Blade Review from the SME Review Queue
  • Once an SME claims a Blade Review, no other SME can work on that Blade Review at the same time
  • Other assigned SMEs can see that the Blade Review has been claimed and by whom
  • The claiming SME can release their own Blade Review claim only before creating Defect Annotations
  • An Admin, Site Manager, or Project Manager can release a Blade Review claim when reassignment is needed
  • If a claimed Blade Review already has Defect Annotations, releasing the claim requires an Admin, Site Manager, or Project Manager
  • The SME who completes the Blade Review is recorded as the accountable reviewer
  • A Blade Review reviews the Blade Evidence from one Blade Inspection
  • A Blade Review is performed by an SME
  • Every required image in a Blade Review has an Image Review Status

  • Review Complete for an image means the SME has reviewed that image, whether or not it has Defect Annotations
  • A Blade Review is complete when every required image has Image Review Status Review Complete
  • Blade Review is performed per blade so the SME accountability boundary matches the submitted Blade Inspection
  • Blade Review Status can be Not Started, In Review, or Review Complete
  • Not Started for a Blade Review means the corresponding Blade Inspection has not been submitted yet
  • In Review begins automatically when the corresponding Blade Inspection is submitted
  • Review Complete means the SME has reviewed and completed all required blade images for that Blade
  • A Completed Blade Review may have zero or more Defect Annotations
  • A Completed Blade Review does not require Findings
  • Defect Annotations do not need to be attached to Findings for the Blade Review to be complete
  • Turbine-level review progress is derived from the Blade Reviews for that Turbine
  • Blade Review Rollup summarizes turbine-level blade review progress for display
  • Blade Review Rollup can be Not Started, In Review, or Review Complete
  • A typical three-blade turbine has review complete when Blade A, Blade B, and Blade C each have a Completed Blade Review
  • Review Complete for a Blade Review Rollup means every expected Blade Review is complete
  • Turbine-level No Findings is a downstream Finding result, not a Blade Review Rollup
  • There is no separate Turbine Review domain object
  • A Work Order is scoped to exactly one turbine
  • A Work Order must link at least one approved Finding
  • A Work Order can group approved Findings across multiple Blades on that Turbine
  • A Work Order can link only Findings whose current state is approved
  • Findings in Changes Requested or Archived Finding cannot be linked to a Work Order
  • A Finding can belong to at most one active Work Order
  • A Work Order can be created by a Site Manager, Project Manager, or Admin
  • Work Order Creation does not require a Project Manager to exist for the Site
  • A Work Order contains exactly one Operational Decision
  • The selected Operational Decision belongs to the Work Order, not the Finding
  • A Finding can carry an SME recommendation, but it does not own the selected Operational Decision
  • If grouped Findings have conflicting SME recommendations, the Work Order creator chooses one Operational Decision and records the rationale
  • If grouped Findings require different Operational Decisions, create separate Work Orders
  • Work Order Status can be Draft, Open, Assigned, In Progress, Blocked, Pending Repair Review, Rework Required, Pending Work Order Verification, Pending Operational Review, Closed, or Archived
  • Assigned, In Progress, Pending Repair Review, and Rework Required apply to physical-work Operational Decisions
  • Pending Operational Review applies to non-repair Operational Decisions such as Monitor, Defer, and Reinspect
  • Pending Work Order Verification means required technical or operational prerequisites are complete and the Work Order is waiting on Work Order Verification
  • Repair Evidence belongs to exactly one Work Order
  • Repair Evidence requires at least one completion photo and a completion note before the Technician can submit physical work
  • Materials used, time spent, and safety notes can be optional Repair Evidence fields unless the field process requires them
  • Repair Review belongs to exactly one Work Order
  • Repair Review is performed by an SME and does not close the Work Order
  • Repair Evidence and Repair Review are required for physical-work Operational Decisions such as repair or replace
  • Non-repair Operational Decisions such as monitor, defer, or reinspect use Closure Evidence instead of Repair Evidence
  • Repair Review is not required for non-repair Operational Decisions unless explicitly requested by the Operational Decision
  • Reinspect creates or schedules a future Blade Inspection with fresh Blade Evidence
  • Monitor keeps a Finding open during a defined review window and does not schedule a new Blade Inspection yet
  • Monitor requires a review date or monitoring window
  • Defer postpones known action for operational reasons
  • Defer requires a defer reason and target date
  • Work Orders with Monitor or Defer decisions remain open as Pending Operational Review until the monitoring window or defer target date is resolved
  • Monitor and Defer Work Orders do not require a Technician assignment unless the Operational Decision later changes to physical work
  • An open Work Order can receive an Operational Decision Change when the same Findings and Turbine context still apply
  • Operational Decision Change keeps audit history of the previous Operational Decision
  • If the changed decision requires a different set of Findings or a different Turbine, create a separate Work Order
  • Repair Review Status can be Pending Review, Repair Review Complete, or Rework Required
  • Repair Review Complete means the SME says the Repair Evidence is technically acceptable and ready for Work Order Verification
  • Rework Required means the SME sends the Work Order back to the Technician with remarks
  • Rework Required returns the Work Order to the same Technician by default
  • A Site Manager, Project Manager, or Admin can reassign a Work Order after Rework Required
  • Work Order Verification cannot happen until Repair Review Status is Repair Review Complete
  • Work Order Verification belongs to exactly one Work Order
  • Work Order Verification is performed by a Site Manager, Project Manager, or Admin
  • Work Order Verification Status can be Accepted or Sent Back
  • Accepted closes the Work Order and can close linked Findings
  • Sent Back returns the Work Order to the appropriate prior state with remarks
  • For physical repair, Sent Back returns the Work Order to the Technician
  • For Monitor, Defer, or Reinspect, Sent Back returns the Work Order to Pending Operational Review or the operational owner
  • Turbine Health Status is derived, not manually edited
  • Turbine Health Status can be Unknown, Healthy, Findings Present, Work In Progress, or Pending Reinspection
  • Unknown means there is not enough completed review data yet
  • Healthy means all required Blade Reviews are complete and there are no open Findings or active Work Orders
  • Findings Present means one or more Findings exist and are not fully resolved
  • Work In Progress means one or more active Work Orders exist
  • Pending Reinspection means a Reinspect decision is scheduled but not completed
  • When multiple Turbine Health Status conditions apply, use this priority: Work In Progress, then Pending Reinspection, then Findings Present, then Healthy, then Unknown
  • A Turbine Health Status can become Healthy only after required Blade Reviews are complete, downstream Findings are resolved, related Work Orders are closed, and required Work Order Verification is accepted
Example Dialogue

Example Dialogue

Dev: "After a Finding is approved, do we create an Action Case?"

Domain expert: "No. Action Case is retired language. Create a Work Order directly from approved Findings, and put the Operational Decision on that Work Order."


Dev: "Does the SME approve the Finding?"

Domain expert: "No. The SME submits the technical claim; Finding Approval is normally a Site Manager decision, with Project Manager or Admin as fallback."


Dev: "If Finding Approval rejects a Finding, is it terminal?"

Domain expert: "No. Use Changes Requested when the Finding should go back to the SME for edits and resubmission."


Dev: "What Finding statuses should we use?"

Domain expert: "Use Draft, Submitted, Changes Requested, Approved, Actioned, Closed, and Archived."


Dev: "What if the Finding is a duplicate or accidental submission?"

Domain expert: "Archive it. Archived Finding is terminal and means the Finding should not continue."


Dev: "Does the Inspector decide which images might contain defects?"

Domain expert: "No. The Inspector captures the complete Blade Evidence. The SME reviews that evidence and creates Defect Annotations. Findings are created downstream."


Dev: "Can the Inspector flag obvious damage as a draft defect?"

Domain expert: "No. Even obvious damage becomes part of the complete Blade Evidence; the SME creates Defect Annotations during Blade Review, and Findings are created downstream."


Dev: "Can the Technician verify the Work Order they just completed?"

Domain expert: "No. The Technician executes the Work Order and uploads Repair Evidence; Work Order Verification is separate operational sign-off."


Dev: "Does the SME verify repair completion?"

Domain expert: "The SME performs Repair Review on Repair Evidence. Work Order Verification is still operational sign-off by Site Manager, Project Manager, or Admin."


Dev: "What do we call the SME reviewing all images for blade B on turbine WTG-014?"

Domain expert: "That is a Blade Review. It may include Defect Annotations, but formal Findings are created downstream."


Dev: "If the SME reviews every image and sees no reportable defect, do we create a Finding?"

Domain expert: "No. The Blade Review is completed, and no Finding is created downstream."


Dev: "Can a healthy Turbine have No Findings?"

Domain expert: "Yes, after the expected Blade Reviews are complete and the downstream Finding path creates no Findings."


Dev: "Is the box drawn around leading-edge erosion the Finding?"

Domain expert: "No. The box is a Defect Annotation. The Finding is the formal defect finding that may reference that annotation."


Dev: "Does every Defect Annotation automatically become a Finding?"

Domain expert: "No. The SME explicitly creates a Finding from selected Defect Annotations."


Dev: "Does every Defect Annotation need to be attached to a Finding before the Blade Review can be completed?"

Domain expert: "No. Blade Review completion is based on completing review of the required images, not on creating Findings."


Dev: "Can one Finding cover Blade A and Blade C on the same Turbine?"

Domain expert: "No. A Finding is blade-specific. A Work Order is turbine-scoped and can group Findings across multiple Blades on that Turbine."


Dev: "Can the same Defect Annotation be used in two Findings?"

Domain expert: "No. One Defect Annotation can belong to at most one Finding."


Dev: "Can one Work Order cover defects on three different turbines?"

Domain expert: "No. A Work Order can group multiple Findings only when they belong to the same turbine."


Dev: "Can one Work Order cover Blade A and Blade C on the same turbine?"

Domain expert: "Yes. A Work Order is turbine-scoped, not blade-scoped."


Dev: "Is a Project Manager assigned to every site?"

Domain expert: "No. A site can exist without a Project Manager. Work Orders can still be created by Site Manager or Admin."


Dev: "Is the Project Manager the site authority?"

Domain expert: "No. The Project Manager coordinates workflow across Managed Sites; the Site Manager is the site-level operational authority."


Dev: "Did the Admin approve the defect?"

Domain expert: "Only as fallback language. Admin is a company-level administrator; normal workflow language should name the operational decision, such as Finding Approval or Work Order Verification."


Dev: "Can Super Admin close a customer's Work Order?"

Domain expert: "No. Super Admin governs the Aflo platform and cross-company setup; customer workflow decisions stay with customer-side roles."


Dev: "Is a Company the same thing as a wind site?"

Domain expert: "No. Company is the tenant boundary. A Company can own sites, and an ISP/O&M Company can manage specific sites owned by another Company."


Dev: "Is a Site the same thing as a project?"

Domain expert: "No. Site means the wind farm or wind power plant. Project is not canonical language for this app right now."


Dev: "Is WTG a different thing from Turbine?"

Domain expert: "No. WTG is the field abbreviation for wind turbine generator. The product term is Turbine."


Dev: "If Findings are on Blade A and Blade C of the same Turbine, do we need two Work Orders?"

Domain expert: "Not necessarily. Findings are blade-specific, but the Work Order is turbine-scoped and can group multiple Blades on the same Turbine."


Dev: "Does Work Order Creation require a Project Manager?"

Domain expert: "No. Site Manager, Project Manager, or Admin can create a Work Order from approved Findings."


Dev: "Can a Work Order include a Finding that is Changes Requested or Archived?"

Domain expert: "No. Work Orders can link only currently approved Findings."


Dev: "Can the same Finding be linked to two active Work Orders?"

Domain expert: "No. A Finding can belong to at most one active Work Order."


Dev: "Can we create a Work Order with no Findings for preventive or ad hoc work?"

Domain expert: "No, not in this workflow. Work Orders are created from approved Findings; preventive or ad hoc maintenance is future scope."


Dev: "What statuses should Work Orders use?"

Domain expert: "Use Draft, Open, Assigned, In Progress, Blocked, Pending Repair Review, Rework Required, Pending Work Order Verification, Pending Operational Review, Closed, and Archived. Physical-work statuses apply only when technician work exists."


Dev: "Does the Operational Decision live on the Finding or the Work Order?"

Domain expert: "Work Order. A Finding can carry an SME recommendation, but the selected Operational Decision belongs to the Work Order."


Dev: "What if grouped Findings have conflicting SME recommendations?"

Domain expert: "The Work Order creator chooses one Operational Decision and records the rationale. If the Findings need different decisions, split them into separate Work Orders."


Dev: "Does every Work Order need Repair Evidence and Repair Review?"

Domain expert: "No. Repair Evidence and Repair Review are for physical work such as repair or replace. Monitor, defer, or reinspect use Closure Evidence unless the Operational Decision explicitly asks for Repair Review."


Dev: "What Repair Evidence is required before Technician submission?"

Domain expert: "At least one completion photo and a completion note. Materials, time spent, and safety notes can be optional unless the field process requires them."


Dev: "What does Reinspect mean?"

Domain expert: "Reinspect means schedule a future Blade Inspection to collect fresh Blade Evidence for the Finding."


Dev: "What does Monitor mean?"

Domain expert: "Monitor means keep the Finding open for a defined review window without scheduling a new Blade Inspection yet."


Dev: "What does Defer mean?"

  • "SME verification" is ambiguous. The SME may perform Repair Review, but Work Order Verification is operational sign-off on a completed Work Order.

  • "Inspection Evidence" is retired. The canonical term is Blade Evidence.

  • "Turbine Review" and Turbine Review Status are not canonical. The meaningful SME review unit is Blade Review; use Blade Review Rollup for turbine-level derived UI status.

  • Blade Inspection Status and Turbine Inspection Status track field evidence capture and Inspector submission only. They do not include No Findings or Findings Present.
  • Turbine Inspection Status and Blade Review Rollup are derived UI summaries, not workflow objects, and they are not semantic mirrors.

  • Repair Review is scoped to physical-work Operational Decisions. Do not force it onto monitor, defer, or reinspect decisions unless the Operational Decision explicitly requires it.
  • Reinspect is not a passive note. It schedules a future Blade Inspection and fresh Blade Evidence.
