Canonical Workflow

This is the workflow both repos should implement and display. Each step has a distinct accountability owner.

  1. Blade InspectionInspector captures required Blade Evidence for one blade on one turbine.
  2. Submit Blade InspectionInspector confirms the evidence is complete and ready for SME review.
  3. Blade ReviewSME reviews every required image and marks Image Review Status as Review Complete.
  4. Defect AnnotationSME marks and classifies reportable evidence regions only where defects exist.
  5. Create FindingSME creates a formal Finding from selected Defect Annotations.
  6. Finding ApprovalSite Manager normally approves; Project Manager and Admin are fallback or coordination roles.
  7. Work OrderOperational owner groups approved Findings on the same turbine into one Work Order.
  8. Operational DecisionWork Order carries repair, replace, monitor, reinspect, defer, or escalate vendor decision.
  9. Repair EvidenceTechnician executes physical work and uploads completion proof.
  10. Repair ReviewSME reviews Repair Evidence and marks complete or rework required.
  11. Work Order VerificationSite Manager, Project Manager, or Admin accepts or sends back the Work Order.
  12. Turbine Health StatusSystem derives turbine condition from review, Finding, Work Order, repair, verification, and reinspection state.

Shared Problem

Wind turbine inspection and repair teams need ATOM to preserve the full evidence-to-repair chain without blurring accountability. Current backend and frontend foundations are useful, but they still combine field capture, SME review, operational approval, repair execution, and final verification in places where the canonical domain model separates them.

User-facing confusion

Operators cannot always tell whether a status means field inspection completion, SME review completion, formal Finding approval, repair completion, or final operational verification.

Audit risk

If Defect Annotations, Findings, Work Orders, Repair Evidence, Repair Review, and Work Order Verification are not linked cleanly, the system cannot produce a defensible operational record.

Backend PRD

The backend should become the source of enforced workflow truth: state machines, permissions, transitions, traceability, audit history, and derived Turbine Health Status.

Backend work to add or correct

Blade Inspection and Review

  • Add first-class Blade Inspection state and per-blade submission.
  • Enforce Required Blade Image Slot completion before submission.
  • Add Inspection Return with required remarks.
  • Start Blade Review automatically after submission.
  • Add SME Review Queue and claim/release behavior.

Finding Traceability

  • Restrict Defect Annotation creation to SME during Blade Review.
  • Restrict Finding creation/submission to SME.
  • Store exact Defect Annotation references on Findings.
  • Enforce one annotation to at most one Finding.
  • Add canonical Finding statuses and legacy status mapping.

Work Orders

  • Add Site Manager and Admin fallback where canonical role rules require them.
  • Keep Work Orders turbine-scoped and require approved Findings.
  • Keep Operational Decision on the Work Order.
  • Add Pending Operational Review and Operational Decision Change.

Repair and Verification

  • Promote proof upload to canonical Repair Evidence.
  • Add SME Repair Review before Work Order Verification.
  • Add Repair Review Complete and Rework Required outcomes.
  • Add Work Order Verification outcomes: Accepted and Sent Back.
  • Derive Turbine Health Status with canonical priority order.

Frontend PRD

The frontend should make accountability visible: who captures, who reviews, who creates Findings, who approves, who executes, who performs Repair Review, and who verifies the Work Order.

Frontend work to add or correct

  • Show Ready to Submit, Inspection Complete, and Returned for Correction.

SME Experience

Operational Experience

Verification and Health

  • Rename final verification to Work Order Verification.
  • Use Accepted and Sent Back outcomes.
  • Add Admin fallback where backend permits it.
  • Display derived Turbine Health Status after backend support lands.

Acceptance Criteria

Non-negotiable language rules

Do not use Action Case, Closure Review, Inspection Note, Turbine Review, Pilot, SME approval, or SME verification as current product language. Historical notes may mention them only to explain that they are retired.

Domain correctness

  • Blade Inspection is field capture and submission.
  • Blade Review is SME review of one blade.
  • Defect Annotation is not a Finding.
  • Finding creation is downstream of Blade Review.
  • Work Order is turbine-scoped.

Role boundaries

  • Inspector captures Blade Evidence and does not create Findings.
  • SME reviews, annotates, creates Findings, and performs Repair Review.
  • Site Manager, Project Manager, and Admin approve Findings and verify Work Orders.
  • Technician executes Work Orders and uploads Repair Evidence.

Status clarity

  • Blade Review terminal status is Review Complete.
  • Finding returns use Changes Requested.
  • Work Order physical work passes through Pending Repair Review.
  • Final verification uses Accepted or Sent Back.

Operational outcome

  • Repair Evidence and Repair Review are separate from Blade Evidence and Blade Review.
  • Monitor, Defer, and Reinspect can remain Pending Operational Review.
  • Turbine Health Status is derived from underlying workflow state.
